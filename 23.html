M: A nonlinear text is an object of verbal communication that is not simply one fixed sequence of letters, words, and sentences but one in which the words or sequence of words may differ from reading to reading because of the shape, conventions, or mechanisms of the text. 1
M: the linear text may be seen as a special case of the nonlinear in which the convention is to read word by word from beginning to end. 1
M: Thus, it is not the plot, or the narrative, or any other well- known poetic unit that will be our definitive agency but the shape or structure of the text itself.
